# Glossary

Plain-language definitions of the terms used in this workbench. Ask the assistant
"what is X" or type /define X to look any of these up. Terms are grouped by topic.

## Documents and deliverables

**Deliverable** — Any document the project has to produce. In this workbench, the deliverables are whatever templates you put in the templates folder.

**Template** — Your normal document (usually Word) with the headings and sections you want. The workbench fills a copy of it with your project information.

**VTP (Validation and Test Plan)** — The plan that says what you will test, how, and what counts as a pass.

**DS (Design Specification)** — A document describing how the system is built: its parts, data flows, and settings.

**SO (System Overview)** — A short description of what the system is, where it runs, and what it connects to.

**Security Plan** — A document describing how the system is kept secure: who can log in, what they can do, and how data is protected.

**RTM (Requirements Traceability Matrix)** — A table that links each requirement to the test that checks it, so you can prove nothing was missed.

**SVR (System Validation Report)** — The final summary at the end of validation that says whether the system is recommended for release.

**SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)** — Your organization's official rules for how work is done. The workbench cites these when you put them in the sops folder.

**Change control** — The formal process for assessing and approving a change to a system that is already validated.

## Requirements and testing

**Requirement** — A single statement of something the system must do, written so it can be tested.

**Acceptance criterion** — The precise, testable wording of a requirement: exactly what must be true for it to pass.

**Test case** — Step-by-step instructions to check that one requirement works, with a clear pass or fail result.

**Traceability** — Being able to follow a requirement all the way through to the test that proves it and the evidence that it passed.

**Coverage** — The percentage of requirements that have at least one test. The workbench can require a target level before closeout.

**Evidence** — The proof that a test was run and passed: a report, a screenshot, or a saved result.

**Defect** — Something that did not work as expected during testing.

## Quality and compliance

**GxP** — Shorthand for "good practice" regulations in regulated industries. Work done under GxP must be documented and defensible to an inspector.

**CSV (Computer System Validation)** — Documented proof that a computer system does what it is supposed to do and will keep doing it.

**GAMP 5** — A widely used guide for validating computer systems in regulated industries.

**21 CFR Part 11** — A US regulation covering electronic records and electronic signatures.

**ALCOA+** — A checklist for trustworthy records. Records should be Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, plus Complete, Consistent, Enduring, and Available.

**ALM (Application Lifecycle Management) tool** — Software used to store and manage requirements, tests, and defects. The workbench can export to it.

**Risk category** — An optional label some organizations use to decide how much validation a system needs. This workbench does not require it and never uses it to decide your deliverables.

**RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)** — Controlling what someone can do in a system based on their assigned role.

**Audit trail** — An automatic record of who did what and when inside a system.

## People and roles

**System Owner** — The person accountable for the system overall.

**System Custodian** — The person responsible for running and maintaining the system day to day.

**SME (Subject Matter Expert)** — A person with deep knowledge of a specific area, technical (TSME) or business (BSME).

**RACI** — A way of listing, for each task, who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.

**Stakeholder** — Anyone with an interest in the project: owners, reviewers, approvers, and users.

## Source documents

**BRD (Business Requirements Document)** — A document describing what the business needs the system to do, written before the technical detail.

**Charter** — A short document that authorizes a project and states its purpose and scope.

**MASTER_CONTEXT** — The single summary the workbench builds from all your project files, so everything is in one place.
